## Disclaimer
Wheatbelt Agronomy Supplies Ltd (WAS Ltd) is a fictional entity created for 
illustrative purposes. All financial inputs are derived from publicly 
available data sources as cited below. Working capital metrics are 
derived from first-principles operational reasoning and publicly 
available industry benchmarks. A real engagement would replace these 
estimates with actual ledger data extracted from the company's 
accounting system. This analysis does not constitute financial or 
investment advice.

## Key Assumptions and Analytical Adjustments

### Gross Margin Adjustment
Elders FY2025 retail products segment gross margin of 12.5% is adjusted downward to 11.5% for - Ltd. The adjustment reflects the absence of backward integration (Elders owns Elders Crop Protection manufacturing), smaller purchasing volumes, no proprietary product range, and a partial offset from advisory services revenue at higher margin.

### Urea Cost Pass-Through Rate (70%)
WAS Ltd is assumed to pass through 70% of the urea cost increase to farmers and absorb 30%. The basis is a smaller distributor with no proprietary products competing directly against Elders and Nutrien Ag Solutions across all five hub locations. Full pass-through risks customer attrition. Partial pass-through is consistent with commodity input distributor behaviour during price-spike periods.

Note: Price acceptance and payment timing represent distinct farmer decisions. Broadacre producers facing a mandatory pre-seeding input purchase may accept higher prices to secure supply while managing cash-flow pressure by extending payment beyond standard terms.

### Blended Urea Price (Stress Case)
The stress case applies a blended urea cost: 50% of annual volume is assumed to be pre-purchased at the 2025 average price (US$422.7/mt), and 50% is assumed to be purchased at the April 2026 spot price (US$856.9/mt). The blended stress price is US$639.8/mt, representing a 51.3% increase from base. This reflects realistic distributor purchasing behaviour rather than applying the full spot price to all inventory.

### Working Capital Metrics
All three working capital metrics (DIO, DSO, DPO) are derived from first-principles operational reasoning supported by published sources as cited. A real engagement would replace these estimates with actual ledger data from the company's accounting system.

### DIO Constant Across Scenarios
DIO is held at 67 days in both the base and stress scenarios. A supply-chain lead-time constraint - minimum two to three weeks of replenishment from Perth port to regional hubs - applies equally regardless of trading conditions. DSO deterioration is the primary working capital stress driver.
